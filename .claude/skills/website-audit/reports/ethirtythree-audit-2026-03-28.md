# Website Audit Report: ethirtythree.com

**Audited:** March 28, 2026
**Auditor:** Professional Web Consultant
**Platform:** Squarespace

---

## Executive Summary

E33 is a professional security and surveillance company founded by a former LAPD detective and 20-year law enforcement veteran, and that compelling backstory comes through on the website. The site is clean, professional, and easy to navigate — but several fixable issues are likely costing the business real inquiries every week, most notably a broken "View All" link on the homepage that sends potential customers to a dead page, and a missing page title that hurts Google search rankings. Addressing the handful of issues outlined in this report could meaningfully increase the number of visitors who turn into paying clients.

---

## What's Working Well

**Strong credibility story.** The About page tells Brian Albonetti's background in a compelling, readable way — former LAPD, 20 years in law enforcement, specialized in burglary investigations targeting high-net-worth clients. This is exactly the kind of trust-building content that converts skeptical visitors. It should be even more prominent across the site.

**Clear contact information everywhere.** The email (balbonetti@ethirtythree.com) and phone number ((650) 400-2879) appear in the footer of every page. Customers never have to hunt for how to reach you.

**Live online booking works.** The "Book now" button leads to a fully functional Acuity Scheduling page where prospects can immediately book a 50-minute consultation with Brian. This is a significant competitive advantage that many security firms lack — self-service booking reduces friction and captures leads even outside business hours.

**Active social media presence.** Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn links are in the header on every page. Social proof matters enormously in security services, where trust is the product.

**Service pages are informative.** Both the Commercial and Residential Surveillance pages have substantive content explaining specific services — 24/7 monitoring, AI-powered drone security (Beehive system), and rapid response. The "What Makes Us Different" section on the Residential page is genuinely strong and differentiating.

**The site loads without errors.** All main navigation pages (Home, About, Commercial Surveillance, Residential Surveillance, Contact, Appointments) load correctly and quickly. No crashes or blank pages on the core pages.

**Contact forms on every page.** Nearly every page includes an embedded contact form so visitors can reach out wherever they land, without navigating elsewhere.

## Critical Issues

These issues are actively costing the business customers right now.

### 1. Broken "View All Services" Link — Sends Customers to a Dead Page

On the homepage, under the "Featured Services" section, there is a "View All" button. Clicking it takes visitors to `ethirtythree.com/services` — a page that does not exist. The visitor sees an error message: "We couldn't find the page you were looking for."

**Why this matters:** This is likely one of the most-clicked buttons on your homepage. A visitor who is interested enough to want to see all your services hits a wall and leaves. This is lost business. The link should either be removed or pointed to an existing page (such as the Contact page or one of the service pages).

**How to fix:** In Squarespace, edit the "View All" button on the homepage and update the link destination to `/contact` or `/appointments`, or create a proper Services page and publish it.

### 2. Missing Page Title — Invisible to Google

The browser tab and Google search results for the homepage show only "E33" — with no description of what the business does. When someone searches Google for "surveillance company" or "security monitoring California," your page title tells Google (and the searcher) nothing useful.

Additionally, there is no meta description at all. Meta descriptions are the one or two sentences shown under your business name in Google results. Without one, Google picks random text from your page, which often looks unprofessional.

**Why this matters:** Better page titles and descriptions directly increase the number of people who click on your website when they find it in search results. It also helps Google understand what you do so it shows your site to the right people.

**How to fix:** In Squarespace, go to Pages > Settings for the homepage, and set the SEO Title to something like: "E33 — Professional Surveillance & Security Services | Worldwide Operations." Add a meta description like: "E33 provides 24/7 surveillance, AI-powered drone security, rapid response, and private investigation services for commercial and residential clients worldwide."

### 3. Residential Surveillance Page Has a Placeholder Title ("Services 3")

The browser tab for the Residential Surveillance page displays "Services 3 — E33" instead of "Residential Surveillance — E33." This is a draft placeholder name that was never updated, and it appears in Google search results.

**Why this matters:** A potential client searching for residential security who finds your site in Google will see "Services 3" — which looks unfinished and unprofessional, and may cause them to skip clicking your link entirely.

**How to fix:** In Squarespace, go to the Residential Surveillance page settings and update the page title from "Services 3" to "Residential Surveillance."

## Recommended Improvements

These are not emergencies but should be addressed within 30 days to improve how the business performs online.

**Add a dedicated "Background & Private Investigations" page.** The homepage mentions this service — "Background & Private Investigations" — and it is clearly a core offering. However there is no dedicated page for it. People searching specifically for private investigator services, background checks, or investigations have nowhere to land and learn more. A single focused page for this service would likely bring in new search traffic.

**Add pricing information or a "starting from" range.** Security services are a considered purchase. Visitors who cannot get a rough sense of cost often bounce without contacting you. Even a "Pricing starts at $X for a consultation" or "Plans from $X/month" removes a major barrier to reaching out.

**Add client testimonials or case studies.** The About page establishes Brian's credentials, but potential clients want to know: has he helped people like me? Even two or three short testimonials ("E33 identified a threat at my warehouse that our old system missed — Brian's team responded within minutes") would significantly boost conversion.

**"Background & Private Investigations" needs to appear in the navigation.** Right now the main navigation only shows: Home, About, Commercial Surveillance, Residential Surveillance, Contact. If you offer private investigations, that service deserves a navigation link so visitors looking for it do not have to discover it by scrolling the homepage.

**Add a phone number link.** The phone number (650) 400-2879 appears in the footer but is displayed as plain text. On mobile devices, it should be a clickable link so visitors can call directly with one tap. This is especially important for a security company where someone may be reaching out urgently.

## Quick Wins

These can each be fixed in under an hour, in Squarespace, by the business owner — no developer needed.

1. **Fix or remove the broken "View All" link on the homepage.** Change it to point to `/contact` or `/appointments`. (5 minutes)

2. **Fix the "Services 3" page title on the Residential Surveillance page.** Rename it to "Residential Surveillance" in page settings. (2 minutes)

3. **Add an SEO title and meta description to the homepage.** Go to Pages > Home > Settings > SEO. Add a descriptive title and a 1–2 sentence description. (10 minutes)

4. **Make the phone number in the footer clickable.** In Squarespace, edit the footer block and wrap the phone number in a link using `tel:6504002879`. (5 minutes)

5. **Add a double period typo fix on the homepage.** The Rapid Response description ends with "...day or night.." — two periods. Small detail, but worth fixing for professionalism. (2 minutes)
