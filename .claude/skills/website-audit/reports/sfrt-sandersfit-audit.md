# Website Audit Report — sfrt.sandersfit.com

**Audited:** March 28, 2026
**Auditor:** Professional Web Consultant
**Site Audited:** https://sfrt.sandersfit.com/home
**Business:** SandersFit Rehab & Therapy — Physical Therapy, Dallas-Fort Worth Area

---

## Executive Summary

SandersFit Rehab & Therapy has a professional-feeling website that clearly communicates the business's focus on sports rehabilitation and elite physical therapy across the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex. The site is functional and loads quickly, but several navigation errors, content inconsistencies, and missed opportunities are likely costing the business new patient bookings every week. With a team of 8 highly credentialed doctors and 6 locations, the website underperforms what this practice deserves.

---

## What's Working Well

**Clear purpose.** Within seconds of landing on the homepage, a visitor understands this is a sports-focused physical therapy clinic serving athletes. That clarity is valuable.

**Strong credibility signals.** The team section features 8 Doctors of Physical Therapy with impressive credentials — Duke, Texas State, Howard University, Oklahoma City Thunder, Texas Rangers, and Dallas Stars connections. This builds enormous trust with potential patients and their parents.

**Compelling patient testimonials.** Three detailed, emotional reviews are prominently displayed and speak directly to the outcomes patients care about most: ACL recovery, post-hip surgery, and post-knee surgery for a teenage athlete. These are persuasive and well-placed.

**Multiple calls to action.** The "CONTACT US TO BOOK" button appears in the top navigation bar and within the main content sections, making it easy for a motivated visitor to take action.

**Active social media presence.** Instagram and TikTok links are present, which are the right platforms for reaching athletes and sports families.

**Broad geographic reach.** Six locations across the metroplex (Dallas, Fort Worth, Frisco, Amarillo, Midlothian, Southlake/Colleyville) and a new location are clearly highlighted — this is a significant competitive advantage.

**Contact form is functional.** The booking form on the Contact page works, includes all the right fields (name, phone, email, preferred location, message), and sets expectations with a "1 business day" response promise.

**Insurance information is mentioned.** The About page notes that SFRT accepts most private insurance and offers a self-pay option. This answers a top question every patient has.

## Critical Issues

These are problems actively costing the business patients and revenue right now.

### 1. The "LOCATIONS" navigation link is broken

**What's happening:** Clicking "LOCATIONS" in the top menu takes visitors to the "About Us" page instead of a Locations page. The dropdown sub-menu with individual location links does work, but only if a visitor knows to hover over the word "LOCATIONS" to reveal it.

**Why this matters:** Someone searching for your nearest location — a person who is ready to book — will click "LOCATIONS," land on the wrong page, and likely give up and call a competitor. This is a direct conversion loss happening every day.

**Fix:** Point the "LOCATIONS" link to a dedicated Locations page (or create one), not the About Us page.

---

### 2. The "Downtown Dallas" location page has no page title

**What's happening:** The Downtown Dallas location page (the business's original flagship location) loads with a completely blank browser tab title. Every other location and main page has a proper title.

**Why this matters:** Page titles are one of the most important factors for showing up in Google searches. Someone searching "physical therapy downtown Dallas" will never find this page organically. It also looks unprofessional when shared via link — the preview will show nothing.

**Fix:** Add a descriptive page title such as "Downtown Dallas Physical Therapy | SandersFit Rehab & Therapy."

---

### 3. Southlake location is missing from the footer

**What's happening:** The footer on every page lists 6 addresses — but the Southlake/Colleyville location (the newest one, prominently labeled "NEW LOCATION!" in the navigation) is not listed in the footer address block. The footer shows the Colleyville address for this location but labels it without the context of "Southlake."

**Why this matters:** The footer is where visitors look when they want quick address information. Prospective Southlake patients may not realize there is a location near them, even after visiting the site.

**Fix:** Ensure the footer location list matches all active locations and clearly labels the Southlake/Colleyville location.

---

### 4. Inconsistent phone numbers create confusion

**What's happening:** The main phone number displayed site-wide in the footer and on the Contact page is 817-271-9022. However, the Southlake/Colleyville location page shows a different number: 817-313-2988. There is no explanation to visitors that each location may have its own line.

**Why this matters:** A prospective patient who calls the main number after visiting the Southlake page (or vice versa) may reach the wrong staff, get confused, or give up entirely. Confusion kills bookings.

**Fix:** Either add a "Southlake direct line" label on the Southlake page, or consolidate to one number if it routes correctly, and ensure consistency everywhere.

---

### 5. Location page URLs look broken and harm Google search rankings

**What's happening:** Each individual location page uses a URL that looks like a database error rather than a real address. For example:
- Downtown Dallas: `/inner-page-2406`
- Fort Worth: `/inner-page-2406-7109`
- Midlothian: `/inner-page-2406-7109-5845-9788`

**Why this matters:** Google ranks pages partly based on their web address. A URL like `/downtown-dallas-physical-therapy` tells Google — and your patients — exactly what the page is about. A URL like `/inner-page-2406` tells them nothing. You are losing local search traffic to competitors with cleaner URLs for every location.

**Fix:** Update each location page URL to something descriptive, such as `/locations/downtown-dallas`, `/locations/fort-worth`, etc. This is a meaningful SEO improvement.

---

### 6. No online booking or scheduling tool

**What's happening:** The only way to book an appointment is to submit a contact form and wait up to 1 business day, or call/text. There is no online scheduler.

**Why this matters:** In 2026, patients — especially athletes and busy sports parents — expect to book online instantly, just like they would a restaurant, a haircut, or a gym class. Every competitor that offers online scheduling has an advantage. This is especially important evenings and weekends when your front desk is closed.

**Fix:** Integrate a scheduling tool such as Jane App, SimplePractice, or similar physical therapy scheduling software. Many have a free tier or are low-cost.

## Recommended Improvements
(Fix within 30 days)

**A. Add photos of your facilities and team.**
The site contains no photographs of your clinics, equipment, or staff in action. For a healthcare business, photos build enormous trust. Patients want to see the environment before they walk in the door — especially parents bringing teenagers for rehab. Even a few professional photos or high-quality smartphone photos would make a significant difference.

**B. Create a dedicated Locations landing page.**
Right now, location information is scattered — some in the footer, some in individual location sub-pages, some on the About page. A single, well-organized Locations page with a map, address, phone number, hours, and contact button for each location would serve patients much better and improve local Google rankings.

**C. Add hours of operation.**
Nowhere on the website does it state what hours any of the clinics are open. This is one of the most common questions people have, and its absence forces patients to call just to find out if you are open. Hours should appear on every location page and on the Contact page.

**D. Fix the spelling inconsistency for Dr. Reichert.**
The doctor's name is spelled "Reichert" in her heading but "Rechert" in the first line of her biography on both the homepage and the Our Team page. This looks careless next to her impressive credentials and may undermine trust.

**E. Clean up excessive blank paragraphs.**
Throughout the doctor bios, there are large blocks of empty space caused by blank paragraph tags left in the content. Dr. Macko's bio has 7 blank lines after it. Dr. Hong's has 11. This makes the page feel unfinished and makes content harder to read, especially on mobile.

**F. Add Google Reviews integration or a link to your Google Business profile.**
The site has three testimonials but no star rating and no link to Google Reviews. Since Google is where most patients will verify your reputation before booking, linking to your Google Business profiles (one per location) adds credibility and makes it easy for happy patients to leave reviews.

## Quick Wins
(Can be fixed in under an hour each)

1. **Fix the LOCATIONS navigation link** — change the URL from `/about-us` to a Locations page or create a simple anchor. Time: 5 minutes.

2. **Add a page title to the Downtown Dallas location page** — type one line of text in your website builder. Time: 5 minutes.

3. **Add the Southlake address to the footer** — copy and paste from the existing location page. Time: 10 minutes.

4. **Add a label clarifying the Southlake direct phone number** — add "Southlake direct:" before 817-313-2988 on that location page. Time: 5 minutes.

5. **Fix Dr. Reichert's name spelling** — change "Rechert" to "Reichert" in the first line of her bio, on both the homepage and the Our Team page. Time: 5 minutes.

6. **Remove the blank paragraphs** from the doctor bios — especially Dr. Macko (7 extra lines) and Dr. Hong (11 extra lines). Time: 15 minutes.

7. **Add your hours of operation** to the Contact page, even if it is just "Mon–Fri 8am–6pm." Time: 10 minutes.
